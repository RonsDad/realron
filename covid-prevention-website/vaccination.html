COVID-19 Vaccination

Key Message: COVID-19 vaccines are safe, effective, and the best way to protect yourself from severe illness, hospitalization, and death.

Why Get Vaccinated?

Personal Protection

Reduces risk of severe illness by up to 90%

Community Immunity

Helps protect vulnerable populations

Variant Protection

Updated vaccines target current variants

Long COVID Prevention

Reduces risk of long-term symptoms

Types of COVID-19 Vaccines

mRNA Vaccines

Pfizer-BioNTech, Moderna

  • Use messenger RNA to teach cells to make spike protein
  • Do not contain live virus
  • Cannot give you COVID-19
  • Updated annually for variants

Protein Subunit Vaccines

Novavax

  • Contains harmless spike protein pieces
  • Traditional vaccine technology
  • Alternative for those who prefer non-mRNA
  • Effective against multiple variants

Vaccination Schedule

Children (6 months - 4 years)

Initial series: 2-3 doses depending on vaccine type

Updated vaccine: At least 2 months after last dose

Children & Teens (5-17 years)

Initial series: 2 doses

Updated vaccine: Annually or as recommended

Adults (18-64 years)

Initial series: 2 doses

Updated vaccine: Annually, especially if at risk

Older Adults (65+ years)

Initial series: 2 doses

Updated vaccine: Annually recommended

May receive additional doses if immunocompromised

Safety & Side Effects

Common Side Effects (Normal)

  • Pain at injection site
  • Fatigue
  • Headache
  • Muscle pain
  • Chills
  • Fever
  • Nausea

These side effects typically resolve within 1-2 days and indicate your immune system is responding.

Safety Monitoring

COVID-19 vaccines have undergone the most intensive safety monitoring in U.S. history:

  • Billions of doses administered globally
  • Continuous monitoring through VAERS, v-safe, and VSD
  • Rare side effects quickly identified and addressed
  • Benefits far outweigh risks for eligible individuals
